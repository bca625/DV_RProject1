The third plot shows the relationships between the fare paid by each passenger, their sex, and whether or not they survived. Fare is left as a continuous value on the y-axis, while sex is set as a discrete value on the x-axis. Passengers are color-coded according to whether they survived the tragedy or not. A 0 (red dot) means that this passenger did not survive, while a 1 (blue dot) indicates that this passenger did survive.

Using the facet grid presents the data in an interesting way. The data suggests that passengers were prioritized not only by sex, but also by class. Female passengers in first class survived at a much higher rate than any other group depicted. Female passengers in second class were also more likely to survive than their male counterparts. In third class, there doesnt seem to be much difference in the survival of males or females.

By applying the SQL query select * from titanic where sex is not null AND age <= 10 to the same facet grid from the plot above we are able to see the survival rates of children under 10 years of age according to their class.

The 6th plot was an idea we came up with ourselves. Women and children first is common saying in regards to maritime disasters. But where did passengers on the Titanic prioritize the elderly? In antitheis to the 5th plot, the plot below shows the survival rate of senior citizens (passengers 60 years or older). The axes and facet grid remain the same from the previous plot, while the SQL query was changed to select * from titanic where sex is not null AND age >= 60.

According to the plot, there werent many elderly women on board the Titanic to begin with. Only three women over 60 years old were on board; thakfully, all of them survived. The old men werent so lucky. Over 20 males over the age of 60 were on board the Titanic when it sank, but only three of them made it out alive. The eldest of all passengers, an 80 year old male, was one of the few fortunate enough to make it out. The sample size is small but men over 60 seemed to have the lowest relative survival rate of all the passengers on the ship.
